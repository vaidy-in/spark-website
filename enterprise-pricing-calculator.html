Deal Parameters

Vanilla vs Premium Vanilla includes core course creation, player, basic quizzes, portal, and roles. Premium adds AI Tutor, evaluation quizzes, live reporting, trainer analytics, segments, and advanced features.

Example: For a sales training program with AI-powered Q&A for learners, choose Premium.
Seat count How many employees will have access to the platform. Volume discounts apply automatically based on bands in Pricing Config.

Example: 500 seats = 500 employees can log in and use the platform.

Contract length How long the customer commits. Short-term options use Pricing Config discounts (1/3/6/12 mo). Multi-year options use the 12-month discount rate.

Example: 12 mo = customer commits for one year. 3 years = 36 months with multi-year discount.

One-time setup fee Optional one-time charge for onboarding, setup, configuration, or training. Added to the total deal value but not included in per-seat or monthly price. Setup fee is Spark only (not part of revenue share) and applies in Year 1. Set to 0 to exclude.

Example: ₹50,000 setup fee = one-time charge for initial setup and configuration - added once to the total deal value, 100% to Spark.

Usage Inputs

SD video processing hours How many hours of standard-quality video (720p or lower) the platform processes each month - converting speech to text, adding chapters, generating notes, and extracting slides. New content is typically uploaded month after month.

Example: 40 hrs = about 40 one-hour training videos (or equivalent) processed each month.
hrs / month
HD video processing hours How many hours of high-definition video (1080p or higher) are processed per month. HD costs more to process than standard video - the Technical Assumptions section has a multiplier that converts HD hours to an equivalent cost.

Example: 10 hrs HD at 5x multiplier = roughly the same processing cost as 50 hrs of standard video.
hrs / month
S3 data transfer (streaming) How many hours of video each employee watches per month on average. Data is sent when learners stream (watch) videos. In practice, most users watch 20-50% of content and rarely rewatch.

Example: 2 hrs = each employee watches about 2 hours of training videos per month on average.
hrs / seat / month
Vanilla tier AI Tutor When Yes, AI Tutor costs are included for Vanilla tier (e.g. if negotiated as part of the deal). When No, AI Tutor applies only to Premium. Use this flag to model scenarios where Vanilla includes AI Tutor.
AI Tutor usage How many questions each learner asks the AI Tutor per month. Applies to Premium always; applies to Vanilla when "AI Tutor included in Vanilla plan?" is Yes.

Example: 20 = each employee asks the AI about 20 questions per month (e.g., clarifying course content, getting help on topics).
queries / seat / month
Monthly video upload rate How many new videos are added to the platform each month. Customers typically keep uploading content month after month.

Or rather Spark automatically detects new recordings in Team/Zoom cloud and nudges the L&D admin to convert them into courses.

Example: 40 = about 40 new training videos are added to the platform each month.
videos / month
Quiz generation (Gemini) How many quiz questions are created per video per month. Trainers and admins create quizzes for each video; learners do not generate their own. Total per month = this value × number of videos per month. Applies to both tiers.

Example: 5 questions × 40 videos = 200 quiz questions generated per month total.
questions / video / month

Minimum cost markup target Target minimum Spark net as a % of total costs. A 90% markup means Spark's net equals 0.9× its costs. Base prices for Vanilla and Premium are auto-set to achieve this markup. Vanilla base price is computed from costs; Premium is set to 50% higher than Vanilla.
